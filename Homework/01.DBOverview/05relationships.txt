Explain the different kinds of relationships between tables in relational databases.
Types of relationships, based on the interconnections primary key-foreign key:
- One to One: One entity is associated with another entity.
Ex: Each employee is associated with one department

- One to Many: One entity is associated with many other entities. 
Ex: A company is associated with all working employees in one branch/office/country.

- Many to One: Many entities are associated with only one entity.
Ex: Many employees are associated with one project.

- Many to Many: Many entities are associated with many other entities. 
Ex: In a company many employees are associated with multiple projects(completed/existing), and at the same time, projects are associated with multiple employees.
